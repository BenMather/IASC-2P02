Dorothy Gale, a young girl living on a farm in Kansas, dreams of escaping her dull life. After a conflict involving her dog Toto, Dorothy runs away but soon returns home—just as a powerful tornado strikes. The storm lifts her house into the air, transporting her to the magical land of Oz. When Dorothy’s house lands, it accidentally kills the Wicked Witch of the East. She is greeted as a hero by the Munchkins and the Good Witch of the North, who gives Dorothy the dead witch’s ruby slippers. However, the Wicked Witch of the West, furious over her sister’s death, vows revenge. Dorothy is told the only way home is to seek help from the mysterious Wizard of Oz in the Emerald City. Along the yellow brick road, Dorothy befriends three companions: the Scarecrow, who wants a brain; the Tin Man, who desires a heart; and the Cowardly Lion, who seeks courage. Together, they journey to meet the Wizard, facing obstacles and the Witch’s attempts to stop them. When they finally reach the Emerald City, the Wizard agrees to help—but only if they defeat the Wicked Witch of the West. During their mission, Dorothy is captured, but she ultimately destroys the Witch by accidentally splashing her with water. Returning to the Wizard, the group discovers he is not a powerful sorcerer, but an ordinary man using tricks and machinery. Nevertheless, he helps Dorothy’s friends realize they already possess the qualities they sought. As for Dorothy, she learns that she can return home by clicking her heels together and repeating, “There’s no place like home.” Dorothy awakens back in Kansas, surrounded by her family, unsure if it was all a dream—but forever changed by her journey.
